Methodology

This study employed a mixed-methods approach combining experimental psychology techniques with neuroimaging to investigate the relationship between cognitive estimation and visual experience in object perception.

Participants

The study included 120 participants (60 male, 60 female) aged 18-45, with normal or corrected-to-normal vision. Participants were recruited through university research pools and community advertisements.

Experimental Design

The study utilized a within-subjects design with the following components:

  • Visual perception tasks using 3D-rendered objects
  • Cognitive estimation tasks measuring response times and accuracy
  • fMRI scanning during task performance
  • Post-experiment interviews and questionnaires

Materials and Apparatus

Stimuli were presented using a high-resolution display system (1920x1080, 60Hz) in a controlled laboratory environment. Neuroimaging data was collected using a 3T MRI scanner with a 32-channel head coil.

Procedure

Participants completed the following sequence:

  1. Informed consent and screening
  2. Practice trials
  3. Main experimental session (2 hours)
  4. Post-experiment debriefing

Data Analysis

Data analysis included:

  • Behavioral response time and accuracy analysis
  • fMRI preprocessing and statistical analysis
  • Correlation analysis between behavioral and neural measures
  • Qualitative analysis of interview responses
