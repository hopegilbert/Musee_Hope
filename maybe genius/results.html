Results

The analysis of our experimental data revealed several key findings regarding the relationship between cognitive estimation and visual experience in object perception.

Behavioral Results

Analysis of response times and accuracy showed significant differences in processing patterns:

  • Mean response time for shape estimation: 450ms (SD = 85ms)
  • Mean response time for material estimation: 520ms (SD = 95ms)
  • Overall accuracy rate: 87% (SD = 6%)

Neuroimaging Results

fMRI data revealed distinct activation patterns:

  • Increased activity in the ventral visual stream during shape perception
  • Stronger activation in the parietal cortex during material property estimation
  • Significant correlation between response times and neural activity patterns

Individual Differences

Analysis of individual performance patterns showed:

  • Three distinct processing strategies among participants
  • Significant correlation between strategy preference and neural activation patterns
  • No significant effect of age or gender on processing patterns

Qualitative Findings

Post-experiment interviews revealed:

  • Participants reported varying levels of awareness of their estimation processes
  • Some participants described a "feeling" of material properties before conscious recognition
  • Others reported a more analytical approach to object perception
