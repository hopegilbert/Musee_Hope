Results

Response Time Analysis

The analysis of response times revealed significant differences between conditions (F(2, 238) = 15.67, p < .001). Participants were faster to make judgments about object shape (M = 450ms, SD = 120ms) compared to material properties (M = 520ms, SD = 140ms).

Eye-Tracking Data

Eye-tracking analysis showed distinct patterns of visual attention for different object properties. Participants spent more time fixating on edges and contours when judging shape (M = 65% of viewing time) compared to material properties (M = 45% of viewing time).

Individual Differences

Significant individual differences were observed in both response times and eye movement patterns. These differences were correlated with participants' prior experience with 3D visualization tasks (r = .42, p < .01).

Neural Correlates

Analysis of the neural data revealed distinct activation patterns in the ventral visual stream for shape perception and material property estimation. These patterns were consistent across participants and showed temporal differences in their onset.
