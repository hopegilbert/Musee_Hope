Introduction

The perception of object shape and material properties is a fundamental aspect of human cognition, yet the precise mechanisms underlying this process remain a subject of ongoing investigation. This dissertation explores the temporal relationship between cognitive estimation and visual experience in the perception of object shape and material properties.

Traditional models of perception have often assumed a sequential process, where cognitive estimation follows visual experience. However, recent research has challenged this assumption, suggesting that these processes may occur independently and concurrently. This study aims to investigate this temporal relationship through a series of carefully designed experiments.

The implications of this research extend beyond theoretical understanding. A clearer comprehension of the temporal dynamics of perception could inform the development of artificial intelligence systems, improve human-computer interaction, and contribute to the advancement of assistive technologies for individuals with perceptual impairments.
